three years later he's one of the best people i've ever worked with.

you didn't go to a school anyone's heard of. maybe you're not in the US. but you've trained models. real ones. not wrappers, not API calls, not tutorials you followed and tweaked. you've stared at loss curves and debugged gradient issues and felt that specific kind of insanity when a run finally converges at 3am and nobody's awake to tell.

you have work but not the resume. you've shipped something and nobody gave you permission to ship it.

this is your post.

here's what i want you to send:
a link to something you trained. github, huggingface, a video, whatever. the thing itself.

a few sentences on what made it hard and what you learned.

that's it. no resume required. no cover letter. just the work and the story behind it.
